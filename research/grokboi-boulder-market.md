## Question 1

**"What are people in Boulder Colorado saying on Nextdoor, Facebook groups, and Reddit about tile installation, bathroom remodels, and handyman work? What are they looking for, what are they complaining about, and what do they typically pay?"**

---

### Grok Response

People in Boulder, CO, frequently discuss these topics on Reddit (especially r/boulder), Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and local review sites like Yelp/Angi/Thumbtack. Demand is high due to the area's older homes, high property values, and homeowners' desire for updates, but labor shortages, permitting complexities (especially in the City of Boulder), and elevated costs lead to common frustrations.

**Sources consulted:** 8 web pages including reddit.com/r/boulder

---

### What People Are Looking For

- **Reliable, licensed, and insured pros** who do quality work without constant oversight. Recommendations often highlight contractors/handymen who coordinate well (e.g., with plumbers), communicate clearly, finish on time, and handle permits.

- **Tile installers:** Experienced setters for showers, floors, backsplashes. General contractors in Boulder post in Facebook groups seeking subs for tile work.

- **Bathroom remodels:** Full gut jobs (down to studs), shower/tub replacements, tile work, and updates in condos or small spaces. Quick or phased work is preferred since many have only one bathroom.

- **Handymen:** For smaller jobs (repairs, installs, misc. projects) or as part of larger remodels. First-time homeowners and those avoiding TaskRabbit seek dependable "go-to" people for tiling, plumbing tie-ins, drywall, etc.

- **Positive mentions often include:** JLB Construction (with Applewood Plumbing for showers/tile), Ashton's Remodeling, Modern Homestead, Gage Home ($120/hr, transparent), and various handyman services on Nextdoor/Thumbtack.

---

### Common Complaints

- **High/"Boulder Tax" costs** — quotes that feel inflated due to strong demand, high living costs for tradespeople, truck/insurance overhead, and material prices. Some suspect markups or that pros avoid small jobs.

- **Difficulty finding takers** — Many contractors are booked or uninterested in small/condo jobs with shared walls and strict permitting. Delays from scheduling/subs/materials are common.

- **Quality and reliability issues** — Poor installs (e.g., tile), unfinished work, or communication breakdowns. Some warn against cheap labor leading to future problems. General frustration with "show" (fancy trucks) over substance.

- **Permitting and local rules** — Boulder/Boulder County can be bureaucratic; out-of-area (e.g., Denver) contractors may skip permits, causing risks.

- **Occasional horror stories** (e.g., disputes leading to damage, though one older CO case went viral and was fixed by other trades).

On Nextdoor and Facebook, posts are mostly recommendation requests with positive local shoutouts; complaints are more scattered in reviews.

---

### Typical Costs (Rough 2024–2026 Figures from Local Discussions)

Costs vary widely by scope, materials (e.g., basic Daltile vs. luxury), finishes, and whether it's labor-only or full-service. Boulder is pricier than national averages.

- **Full bathroom remodel** (e.g., ~50 sq ft, down to studs): **$20k–$30k+** common. One poster was quoted $25k and found it reasonable after research (others noted it could be "cheap" depending on finishes). Materials alone can hit $7–11k+. Stand-up shower replacements around $12k in nearby areas.

- **Tile installation:** Labor often **$30–$40+/sq ft** in the broader Denver area (varies). Shower floor projects $1k–$2.5k mid-range. Full tile work adds significantly to remodels.

- **Handyman rates:** **$75–$135+/hour** (e.g., $120/hr for some transparent services with no hidden fees). Small jobs have minimums; full remodels scale up.

**Tips from locals:** Get multiple quotes, check references/insurance, consider splitting trades (e.g., plumber separate), or DIY parts if handy. RTA cabinets and mid-range materials help control costs. Demand is high, so book early.

**Overall sentiment is mixed:** Appreciation for skilled local work but sticker shock and hunting challenges are frequent. Check r/boulder, Nextdoor "Handyman" or "Bathroom Remodeling" topics, and Boulder-specific Facebook groups for the latest recs. Always verify licensing.
