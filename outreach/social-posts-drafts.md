### NEXTDOOR POSTING TIPS

**Which neighborhoods to post in:**
- Mapleton Hill, Whittier, Newlands, North Boulder — older homes, high income, bathroom remodels are common
- Table Mesa, Martin Acres, Chautauqua — established neighborhoods, homeowners invest in updates
- University Hill, Baseline — condo/townhome owners doing bathroom refreshes

**Category:** Post under "Recommendations" or "Home & Garden" — NOT "For Sale." The Home & Garden category is where people ask for and share contractor recommendations.

**Tone rules:**
- Nextdoor is hyper-local and personal. Write like a neighbor, not a business. No hard sell.
- First person ("I'm Kevin" not "Miles of Tile Styles LLC offers...")
- Keep it short — 4–6 lines max. People scroll fast on Nextdoor.
- Don't include a link to a website on the first post — Nextdoor deprioritizes posts with external links.
- DO include the phone number directly in the post.

**Engagement tip:** After posting, watch for any "looking for a tile person" or "bathroom remodel recommendation" posts in your feed. Reply to those with a brief personal response — that's where real leads come from on Nextdoor.

**Frequency:** Don't post the same thing every week. One intro post, then engage naturally by responding to others' questions. You can post again in 3–4 weeks with a project photo/update.

**Photos:** Attach 1–2 photos. Nextdoor posts with images get significantly more views. One stunning completed shower is better than five mediocre shots.

### CRAIGSLIST POSTING TIPS

**Renewal:** Craigslist posts drop off the front page within 2–3 days. Renew the post every 48 hours (Craigslist allows you to renew from your account without reposting). Repost fresh every 7–10 days with slight title variations.

**Title variations for reposting:**
- "Tile Installer — 30 Years, Wedi Certified — Bathroom Remodels — Boulder"
- "Bathroom Remodel Specialist — Custom Tile, Heated Floors — Boulder CO"
- "Custom Shower & Tile Work — 30 Yrs Experience — Free Estimates — Boulder"

**Photos:** Add 3–5 portfolio photos to the Craigslist post. Posts with images get 3–4x more clicks. Same advice as Facebook — lead with the best shower.

**Price indicator:** Craigslist has optional pricing fields. Don't put a price — put "Free Estimates" or leave it blank. You don't want to anchor low or scare people off.

**Location:** Set the map pin to downtown Boulder so it shows up for anyone searching the Boulder area.

**Do NOT:**
- Don't post in multiple categories — Craigslist flags that as spam
- Don't put ALL CAPS in the title — one or two capitalized words max
- Don't include a URL in the body (Craigslist sometimes flags external links)
- Don't use the word "cheap" — Kevin is premium, price accordingly
