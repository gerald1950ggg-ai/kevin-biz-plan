# Miles of Tile Styles LLC — The 10-Year Road
**Kevin Gardner · Boulder, Colorado**
**Prepared:** May 25, 2026

---

## The Vision

Kevin Gardner has 30 years of high-end tile craftsmanship. He's back in Boulder to rebuild — not just a business, but a life. The goal isn't just revenue. It's owning a home, building something that lasts, and eventually retiring on his own terms with financial freedom.

He also rides a Ducati through the mountains, photographs street art, and creates mosaics. This isn't a guy who just lays tile. He's an artist who happens to work in stone and porcelain. That matters for where this goes.

---

## Phase 1: LAUNCH (Months 1–6 · 2026)
**Revenue target: $75K first half → $150K full year**

### What Happens
- Land first paying clients through Nextdoor, Facebook, Craigslist — direct to homeowner
- Walk into tile shops (Crossville Studios, Star Flooring) with Wedi cert and portfolio
- Email/call design-build firms (Melton, Factor, Tectonic) — get on their sub list
- Build relationships with interior designers (Joe McGuire, Emily Tucker, Pocci)
- Gerald builds a simple one-page website with portfolio
- Kevin does the work himself — every job is a portfolio piece and a referral source

### Milestones
- [ ] First deposit by end of Week 1 (May 29)
- [ ] 3 completed projects by end of Month 1
- [ ] On referral lists at 2+ tile shops by Month 2
- [ ] First design-build subcontract by Month 3
- [ ] Steady 2–3 projects per month by Month 4
- [ ] Website live with 10+ portfolio photos by Month 2
- [ ] $150K gross revenue by end of Year 1

### Revenue Math
- Average project: $3,000–$8,000 (Kevin's labor on a mid-range bathroom)
- At 3 projects/month avg = ~$15K/month = $180K run rate
- Conservative (accounting for ramp-up): $150K Year 1

### Key Metric
**Referral rate.** Every job should generate at least one warm lead. If Kevin finishes a bathroom and the homeowner tells nobody, something went wrong.

## Phase 2: MOMENTUM (Year 1–2 · 2027)
**Revenue target: $200–250K**

### What Happens
- Full schedule — Kevin is booking 3–4 weeks out consistently
- Designer/architect referral pipeline is flowing — these become recurring sources
- Expand service area: Longmont, Lafayette, Louisville, Erie (30-min radius)
- First hire — bring on one experienced tile installer Kevin trusts
- Raise rates. Boulder market supports $40+/sq ft for premium work. Kevin's Wedi cert and portfolio justify premium pricing.
- Start tracking: cost per lead by channel, close rate, average project size, referral rate

### Milestones
- [ ] Booked 3+ weeks out consistently
- [ ] 3+ design firms sending regular work
- [ ] First employee or 1099 installer onboarded
- [ ] Service area expanded beyond Boulder proper
- [ ] Gross revenue $200–250K

### Key Metric
**Booking depth.** If Kevin is less than 2 weeks booked out, something needs attention. If he's 4+ weeks out, it's time to hire.

---

## Phase 3: TEAM (Years 3–5 · 2028–2030)
**Revenue target: $350–500K+**

### What Happens
- Team of 2–3 installers working under Kevin's quality standard
- Kevin transitions from 100% on tools to 50/50 — estimating, client relationships, quality control
- Larger projects: full master suite renovations, multi-bathroom homes, small commercial (restaurant, boutique hotel, spa)
- **Mosaic art specialty** — Kevin's artistic eye becomes a premium differentiator. Custom mosaic work for feature walls, entries, pools. This is where art meets trade.
- Consider: small office/shop space for material staging and client meetings
- Build a real brand: professional photography of completed work, case studies, Google reviews target 50+

### Milestones
- [ ] 2–3 installers on payroll or reliable 1099
- [ ] Kevin spending <50% time on tools
- [ ] First commercial project completed
- [ ] Custom mosaic art project completed (portfolio piece)
- [ ] Revenue $350–500K
- [ ] 50+ Google reviews, 4.8+ stars

### Key Metric
**Revenue per Kevin-hour.** As Kevin moves off tools and into management/sales, revenue per hour of his time should increase. If he's making the same managing as he was installing, the team isn't working.

## Phase 4: OWN (Years 5–7 · 2030–2032)
**Revenue target: $500K+ · Personal goal: buy a home**

### What Happens
- **Buy a home.** This is a life milestone, not just a business one. Boulder is expensive — but by Year 5, Kevin should have the income and credit history. Surrounding areas (Longmont, Lafayette, Lyons) are options too.
- Business is established. Referral network is deep. Design firms, architects, and tile shops think of Miles of Tile Styles first.
- Consider a showroom — even a small one. A place where clients can see Kevin's work, touch samples, and trust the brand.
- Start building retirement savings seriously. SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) — Kevin can shelter significant income.
- Premium brand recognition: Kevin is known in Boulder County as THE tile guy. Not one of many — the one.

### Milestones
- [ ] Home purchased
- [ ] Retirement account funded ($50K+ accumulated)
- [ ] Business showroom or dedicated office space
- [ ] Revenue sustained at $500K+
- [ ] Kevin spending <30% of time on tools

### Key Metric
**Personal net worth growth.** The business exists to fund a life. Track it.

## Phase 5: LEGACY (Years 8–10 · 2033–2036)
**Revenue target: sustained · Personal goal: freedom**

### What Happens
- Kevin is the owner, not the primary installer. He does the projects he wants — the custom mosaics, the artistic pieces, the high-end design collaborations.
- The team runs day-to-day installation work. Kevin trains, quality-checks, and picks the interesting jobs.
- **Public art and mosaic projects.** Boulder has an active public art program ($40K commissions for bridge mosaics, etc.). Kevin's 35+ years of tile + his mosaic artistry makes him a legitimate candidate. This is where the Ducati-riding, street-art-loving side of Kevin becomes a business asset.
- Financial freedom. Mortgage is being paid. Retirement fund is growing. Kevin can choose to work, not have to.
- Retire on his own terms. Not forced out. Not broken down. Riding his Ducati through the mountains with nothing hanging over him.

### Milestones
- [ ] Kevin on tools by choice, not necessity
- [ ] Public art/mosaic commission completed
- [ ] Mortgage ahead of schedule
- [ ] Retirement fund on track ($200K+ accumulated)
- [ ] Business can run a full week without Kevin on site

### Key Metric
**Days of freedom.** How many days per month can Kevin ride his Ducati, photograph street art, and not think about work? That number should be growing every year.

## The Intangibles

### Kevin's unfair advantages
1. **30 years.** You can't fake experience. When Kevin walks into a bathroom and sees the substrate, he knows what needs to happen. That's not a skill — it's pattern recognition earned over three decades.
2. **Wedi PRO certification.** Most installers aren't certified in anything. Kevin has a credential that design firms and tile shops specifically look for.
3. **He does the work himself.** In a market full of crews who flip subcontractors, Kevin showing up personally is a massive trust signal.
4. **He's an artist.** The mosaic work, the geometric layouts, the street art eye — this isn't just craft, it's creative vision. That matters to the Boulder clientele.
5. **He gives a damn.** You can hear it in his voice. "Nothing too small, nothing too large." He wants to do good work for real people. That's magnetic.

### The Boulder market advantage
- Demand outstrips supply. Best competitors are booked months out.
- Homeowners pay premium and know it. $20–30K bathroom remodels are normal.
- High ROI on bathroom work (88–95% at resale). Homeowners invest.
- Design-forward community that values craftsmanship and sustainability.
- Public art programs and mosaic commissions — a natural extension for Kevin's skills.

## What Could Go Wrong

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Slow start — no leads in first 2 weeks | Multiple channels at once. Nextdoor + shops + Craigslist. Cast wide. |
| Kevin gets too busy too fast, quality slips | Don't overbook. Better to do 2 excellent jobs than 4 mediocre ones. |
| First hire doesn't meet Kevin's standard | Hire slow. Kevin works with them on the first 3 jobs side by side. |
| Health/injury — Kevin can't work | Insurance. Savings buffer. Team-building is the long-term answer. |
| Boulder market downturn | Diversify: expand area, add commercial, offer maintenance contracts. |
| Pricing pressure from cheaper competitors | Never compete on price. Compete on quality, reliability, and trust. |
