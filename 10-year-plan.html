The Vision
Kevin Gardner has 30 years of high-end tile craftsmanship. He's back in Boulder to rebuild — not just a business, but a life. The goal isn't just revenue. It's owning a home, building something that lasts, and eventually retiring on his own terms with financial freedom.

He also rides a Ducati through the mountains, photographs street art, and creates mosaics. This isn't a guy who just lays tile. He's an artist who happens to work in stone and porcelain. That matters for where this goes.

The Intangibles

Kevin's Unfair Advantages

Five things no competitor can replicate, buy, or shortcut.

01
30 Years.
You can't fake experience. When Kevin walks into a bathroom and sees the substrate, he knows what needs to happen. That's not a skill — it's pattern recognition earned over three decades.
02
Wedi PRO Certification.
Most installers aren't certified in anything. Kevin has a credential that design firms and tile shops specifically look for. It's a filter that lets him skip the crowded bottom of the market.
03
He Does the Work Himself.
In a market full of crews who flip subcontractors, Kevin showing up personally is a massive trust signal. Clients who've been burned before notice immediately.
04
He's an Artist.
The mosaic work, the geometric layouts, the street art eye — this isn't just craft, it's creative vision. That matters to the Boulder clientele. It's how $8K jobs become $20K jobs.
05
He Gives a Damn.
"Nothing too small, nothing too large." He wants to do good work for real people. That's magnetic. And it shows in every referral, every repeat client, every review.
The Boulder Market Advantage
  • Demand outstrips supply — best competitors are booked months out
  • Homeowners pay premium and know it — $20–30K bathroom remodels are normal
  • High ROI on bathroom work (88–95% at resale) — homeowners invest without hesitation
  • Design-forward community that values craftsmanship and sustainability
  • Public art programs and mosaic commissions — a natural extension of Kevin's artistry
Eyes Open

What Could Go Wrong

Every risk is known. Every risk has a response.

Risk Mitigation
Slow start — no leads in first 2 weeks Multiple channels at once. Nextdoor + shops + Craigslist. Cast wide. Don't wait to pick a favorite.
Kevin gets too busy, quality slips Don't overbook. Better to do 2 excellent jobs than 4 mediocre ones. Reputation is the only real asset in Year 1.
First hire doesn't meet Kevin's standard Hire slow. Kevin works with them on the first 3 jobs side by side. Standards are non-negotiable.
Health / injury — Kevin can't work Insurance. Savings buffer. Team-building is the long-term structural answer.
Boulder market downturn Diversify: expand area, add commercial, offer maintenance contracts.
Pricing pressure from cheaper competitors Never compete on price. Compete on quality, reliability, and trust. The client who shops on price is not Kevin's client.
